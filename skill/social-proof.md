# Building Social Proof Before You Apply

Grant reviewers Google you. Your X profile, GitHub, and Superteam Earn page are read before your application. If they're empty, your application starts at a disadvantage regardless of how good the idea is.

This file covers how to build a visible track record in the Solana ecosystem — specifically the kind that makes grant reviewers say "okay, this person can execute."

---

## The three profiles that matter

### 1. GitHub
What reviewers look for:
- Commits in the last 90 days (shows you're actively building)
- At least one Solana-related repo
- Clean READMEs (shows you can communicate)
- Stars or forks (shows others find your work useful)

What to do if your GitHub is empty:
- Start with small contributions — fix a typo in a Solana docs repo, open an issue, submit a PR to an open-source Solana tool
- Upload your personal projects even if rough — a rough repo is better than no repo
- Pin your most relevant repos to your profile

### 2. Superteam Earn Profile
What reviewers look for:
- Completed submissions (not just started)
- At least one win or honorable mention
- Profile filled out with bio, skills, and links

What to do:
- Complete your profile fully before applying for anything
- Submit to at least 3 bounties before applying for a grant
- Aim to win one — even a small $100 bounty win on your profile counts

### 3. X (Twitter)
What reviewers look for:
- Posts about what you're building (build in public)
- Engagement with Solana ecosystem accounts
- Evidence you understand the space (not just price talk)

What to do:
- Post about the problem you're solving before you apply
- Tag relevant ecosystem accounts when you ship something
- Reply thoughtfully to posts from Foundation, Superteam, Colosseum teams

## The build-in-public playbook

Building in public is the fastest way to create social proof. Here's what to post and when:

### While you're in the idea stage
- "I've been noticing [specific problem] in the Solana ecosystem. Here's what I see happening: [thread]"
- "Talking to [X] developers this week about [problem]. What I'm hearing: [observation]"
- "Starting to build [project name]. Here's the problem I'm trying to solve: [1 sentence]"

### While you're building
- "Week [X] update on [project]: shipped [specific thing]. Here's what it looks like: [screenshot/GIF]"
- "Ran into [specific technical problem] building on Solana. Here's how I solved it: [thread]"
- "First version is live: [link]. Would love feedback from anyone building [related thing]"

### After you ship
- "Shipped [project]. Here's what it does, why I built it, and what I learned: [thread]"
- "Applied for a [Superteam/Foundation] grant for [project]. Posting the application publicly here: [link]"

## The minimum viable social proof

Before submitting any grant application, you should have:

- [ ] GitHub with at least 1 Solana-related repo (even a rough prototype)
- [ ] Superteam Earn profile with at least 2-3 completed submissions
- [ ] At least 3 X posts mentioning the problem you're solving
- [ ] One piece of public proof that someone else cares (a reply, a RT, a GitHub star)

This is the minimum. More is better. None of it is negotiable for competitive programs like Solana Foundation.

---

## How to build social proof fast (2-week sprint)

If your application deadline is close and your profiles are thin:

**Day 1-3:**
- Fill out Superteam Earn profile completely
- Submit to one active Superteam bounty (any category)
- Push your project prototype to a public GitHub repo with a clean README

**Day 4-7:**
- Post a Twitter thread about the problem you're solving (5-7 tweets, specific, with numbers)
- Engage with 5 posts from Superteam, Colosseum, or Solana Foundation accounts — genuine replies, not spam

**Day 8-11:**
- Submit to a second Superteam bounty
- Post a "building update" tweet with a screenshot or demo GIF
- Add your GitHub and X links to every profile you have

**Day 12-14:**
- Finalize your grant application with all profile links included
- Check that all links work and go to the right place
- Submit

---

## The shortcut that isn't a shortcut

Some founders try to bulk-follow ecosystem accounts, post generic "building on Solana 🚀" content, and call it social proof. Reviewers see through this immediately.

Real social proof is specific:
- A thread that shows you understand a problem deeply
- A GitHub commit that shows you can build
- A Superteam submission that shows you can deliver

Fake social proof is generic:
- 500 followers with no engagement
- GitHub with forked repos and no commits
- X profile with price speculation and no original insight

Build the real thing. It takes 2 weeks, not 2 months.
