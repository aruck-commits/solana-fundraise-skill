# Positioning Your Project for Grants

How you frame your project is as important as what you're building. The same product, positioned differently, can go from rejection to funded. This file covers how to find your angle and communicate it clearly.

---

## The three positioning questions

Before writing a single word of your application, answer these:

**1. Who specifically benefits from this?**
Not "Solana builders." Be precise: "Solana founders launching tokens on pump.fun who need X" or "Superteam members in Southeast Asia who do Y."

The narrower your answer, the stronger your application reads.

**2. What's the one thing your project does that nothing else does?**
This is your differentiation. If you can't answer this clearly, reviewers won't be able to either.

**3. Why does the ecosystem need this now?**
Timing matters. What changed in the Solana ecosystem that makes this the right moment to build this?

---

## The four grant positioning archetypes

Pick the one that fits your project and lean into it:

### 1. Infrastructure / Public Good
"I'm building something that makes Solana better for every builder."
- Strongest fit for Solana Foundation
- Frame around ecosystem-wide benefit, not personal gain
- Example: "Every developer who deploys on Solana will benefit from this indexer"

### 2. Regional Ecosystem Builder
"I'm growing Solana in a market that's currently underserved."
- Strongest fit for Superteam regional grants
- Frame around your local context — what's unique about builders in your region
- Example: "India has 2M+ developers but less than 1% are building on Solana. This changes that."

### 3. Developer Experience
"I'm removing a specific friction that slows down Solana builders."
- Works for both Foundation and Superteam
- Frame around time saved / errors reduced / things that currently break
- Example: "This removes 3 hours of manual setup from every new Solana project"

### 4. Ecosystem Activation
"I'm turning potential builders into active contributors."
- Works well for Colosseum and Superteam
- Frame around onboarding, education, community
- Example: "We've already onboarded 200 builders through IRL events — this scales that"

## How to differentiate from similar projects

Grant reviewers Google your idea. If they find 3 similar projects and your application doesn't address them, you look like you didn't do your research.

Do this before you write:
1. Search "Solana [your category]" on GitHub, X, and Superteam Earn
2. Find the 2–3 most similar things that exist
3. In your application, name them and explain specifically why your approach is different

This is called a competitive landscape and it signals seriousness.

Template:
```
Existing solutions like [X] and [Y] solve part of this problem, but neither addresses [specific gap].
[Project name] is different because [specific technical or strategic reason].
```

## How to position at different stages

### You have an idea only
Don't pretend you have more than you do. Lean into:
- The depth of the problem (you've researched it thoroughly)
- Your personal experience with the problem
- The speed at which you can move (short milestone 1: 2 weeks)
- Prior work that shows you can execute

### You have a prototype
This is the sweet spot. Lead with:
- What the prototype already does (link it)
- What you learned from building it
- What's left to make it production-ready

### You have a live product
You're now de-risked. Lead with:
- Usage numbers (even small ones are real)
- User feedback quotes
- What the grant unlocks that you can't do without it

---

## Red flags that kill positioning

- "We are the first to ever do X" — reviewers are skeptical of this claim; prove it
- "The market is huge" — everyone says this; show you understand your specific users
- "This will change everything" — overpromising; be specific about what changes
- Copying the tone and structure of another project's application — reviewers notice
- No mention of what exists already — looks like you didn't research

---

## One-liner formula

Before writing anything else, nail this sentence:

**"[Project name] is a [what it is] that helps [specific user] [specific action] so they can [outcome]."**

Example: "SolFund is a grant readiness tool that helps Solana builders structure milestone-based proposals so they can get funded faster and deliver more reliably."

If you can't write this sentence clearly, your positioning isn't ready yet.
