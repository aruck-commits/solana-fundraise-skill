# After You Get Funded

Getting the grant is step one. Keeping it — and using it to unlock the next one — is the real game. Most founders fumble the post-funding phase. Here's how not to.

---

## The first 48 hours after approval

1. **Confirm the agreement in writing** — email or message the grant contact confirming the milestone structure, payment amounts, and delivery dates. Don't assume verbal is enough.
2. **Set up a dedicated wallet** — keep grant funds separate from personal funds. Makes reporting clean.
3. **Post publicly** — tweet that you got funded. Tag the program. This builds accountability and gets you on their radar as someone who ships.
4. **Create a private progress doc** — a simple Google Doc or Notion page with your milestones, due dates, and a notes column. Update it weekly.

---

## How to deliver milestones

### Before the deadline
- Deliver 3–5 days early whenever possible. Late = friction. Early = trust.
- Prepare your proof before submitting: GitHub link, live URL, screenshot, user count — whatever the milestone requires.
- Write a 2–3 sentence summary of what you built and what you learned. Send it with the delivery.

### The delivery message format
```
Hi [Name],

M[X] delivery for [Project Name]:

What I built: [1 sentence]
Proof: [link]
What's next: [1 sentence on M[X+1]]

Any questions, happy to jump on a call.

[Your name]
```

Short. Specific. Link front and center.

### If you're going to be late
Tell them before the deadline, not after. One message:
```
Hi [Name], heads up — M[X] is running [X] days behind because [specific reason].
New delivery date: [date]. Everything else on track.
```

Programs are human. They handle honest early warnings well. They handle radio silence badly.

## How grant payments work

Most Solana ecosystem grants are milestone-gated:

- **Superteam:** Payment usually within 1–2 weeks of milestone approval
- **Solana Foundation:** Formal review process, can take 2–4 weeks per milestone
- **Colosseum:** Prize payments are faster, typically within days of announcement

If payment is delayed more than 2 weeks past milestone approval with no communication — follow up once, politely. If still nothing after another week, escalate to the program's main Discord or X account.

---

## What to do if you miss a milestone

Missing a milestone happens. How you handle it determines whether you keep the grant.

**Do:**
- Tell them immediately — before the deadline
- Explain the specific reason (not "it was harder than expected")
- Propose a revised timeline with a new date
- Deliver partial proof if you have it

**Don't:**
- Go silent
- Ask for more money in the same message
- Miss two milestones in a row without communication

One missed milestone, communicated early, rarely kills a grant. Two missed milestones with silence almost always does.

## How to use this grant to unlock the next one

Every grant you complete is proof of work for the next application. Do this after every milestone:

1. **Document it publicly** — GitHub commit, tweet, blog post
2. **Add it to your Superteam Earn profile**
3. **Update your bio** — "Funded by [Program]" is a credibility signal
4. **Ask for a quote** — if the grant reviewer is happy with your work, ask if they'd be willing to give a one-line endorsement for your next application

The best grant applications have a section that says "Previously funded by X, delivered Y on time." Build toward that.

## Building toward the next grant

| Current grant | Natural next step |
|---|---|
| Superteam bounty win | Superteam grant application |
| Superteam grant | Solana Foundation application |
| Solana Foundation grant | Colosseum hackathon / accelerator |
| Colosseum prize | Colosseum accelerator / VC outreach |

Each one funds the next. Plan for it before you finish the current one.

---

## The one thing most funded founders skip

**Write a public retrospective when you finish.**

A 500-word thread or blog post: what you built, what you learned, what you'd do differently. Post it, tag the program, link the GitHub.

This does three things:
1. Closes the loop with the grant program (they love this)
2. Gets you discovered by the next program's reviewers
3. Shows future collaborators you can execute and reflect

Nobody does this. Do it.
