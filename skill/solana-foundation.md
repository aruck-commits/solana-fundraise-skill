# Solana Foundation Grants

The most prestigious grant in the Solana ecosystem. Competitive, milestone-based, and heavily weighted toward technical public goods and censorship resistance.

---

## What they fund

The Foundation's grants program focuses on **public goods** — things that make Solana better for everyone, not projects that are primarily commercial.

**Strong fit:**
- Open-source developer tooling (SDKs, CLIs, testing frameworks)
- Infrastructure (RPC improvements, indexers, explorers)
- Security research and auditing tools
- Education and documentation (if technically deep and open)
- Community growth and user onboarding
- Mainnet deployments with ecosystem-wide benefit
- Novel proofs of concept that push the ecosystem forward

**Weak fit / usually rejected:**
- Projects primarily commercial in nature (consider convertible grants instead)
- Consumer apps without a clear public goods angle
- Marketing-only projects
- Anything that competes directly with existing well-funded Solana projects
- Teams with no visible Solana ecosystem contribution

**Note on commercial projects:** The Foundation has a "convertible grant" path for projects that are more commercial. If your project is commercial but ecosystem-relevant, ask about this option when applying.

---

## How to apply

The Foundation has a formal application form at **solana.org/grants-funding**. After submitting:
1. The team reviews your application on a rolling basis
2. They may ask for more information
3. If strong, they schedule a call with your team
4. Approved applicants work with the Foundation Legal Team to finalize the grant agreement
5. Funding is milestone-based — released as you hit deliverables

Rolling deadlines — no fixed windows. Apply when you're ready.

---

## Application structure

### Section 1: Public goods case
- What specific public good does this create?
- Who benefits and how many people/projects?
- Is it open-source? Which parts?
- Why does this further decentralization or censorship resistance?

### Section 2: Problem + Solution
- What's broken right now? (specific, with numbers)
- What are you building exactly?
- How does it work technically? (no buzzwords)
- What does success look like in 3–6 months?

### Section 3: Team
- GitHub links with Solana contributions
- Prior projects shipped on Solana
- Ecosystem contributions (Discord, forums, open-source PRs)
- Any advisors or ecosystem connections?

### Section 4: Milestones
Structure every milestone as: **Name → Verifiable Deliverable → Timeline → Amount**

| Milestone | Deliverable | Timeline | Amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| M1: Core build | Public GitHub repo, working alpha, README | Week 6 | $X |
| M2: Testing + docs | Full docs site, test coverage, 3 integrations | Week 12 | $X |
| M3: Mainnet + adoption | Live on mainnet, X projects using it | Week 20 | $X |

### Section 5: Budget
Be specific. Break it down by milestone and role. Vague budget = pushback.

## What makes a Foundation application stand out

- **Prior ecosystem contributions** — answered questions, filed issues, shipped open-source code
- **Solana-native understanding** — shows you know the accounts model, rent, compute units
- **Realistic milestones** — 3-month plans, not 6-month vague roadmaps
- **Public proof of work** — GitHub, Superteam bounty wins, hackathon placements
- **Clear public goods case** — not "we're building on Solana" but "here's what the whole ecosystem gains"

---

## Common rejection reasons

- No prior Solana ecosystem activity visible anywhere
- Milestones are vague ("complete development", "launch product")
- Budget doesn't match scope
- Problem already solved by a funded project
- Project is primarily commercial with no public goods angle
- Application reads like it was written in 10 minutes

---

## Timeline

- Submit → First response: 2–4 weeks (rolling review)
- First response → Decision: 2–4 more weeks
- Decision → First payment: 1–2 weeks
- Subsequent payments: milestone-gated

Apply to Superteam simultaneously. Don't wait on Foundation before starting.
