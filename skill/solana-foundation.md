# Solana Foundation Grants

The most prestigious grant in the Solana ecosystem. Competitive, milestone-based, and heavily weighted toward technical public goods.

---

## What they fund

**Strong fit:**
- Developer tooling (SDKs, CLIs, testing frameworks)
- Infrastructure (RPC improvements, indexers, explorers)
- Open-source protocols (DeFi primitives, token standards)
- Security research and auditing tools
- Education and documentation (if high-quality and technically deep)
- Localisation and regional developer growth

**Weak fit / usually rejected:**
- Consumer apps with token mechanics
- Marketing or community-only projects
- Anything that competes with existing well-funded Solana projects
- Projects where the team has no prior Solana experience

---

## Application structure

The Foundation doesn't have a single public form — contact is through their grants portal or direct outreach to ecosystem team members. Either way, your application needs to clearly answer:

### Section 1: Problem
- What specific problem does this solve?
- Who is affected and how many people/projects?
- Why hasn't this been solved already?

### Section 2: Solution
- What are you building, exactly?
- How does it work technically (high level)?
- What does success look like in 6 months?

### Section 3: Team
- Who are you? GitHub links, prior projects, ecosystem contributions
- Have you shipped anything on Solana before?
- Do you have any advisors or ecosystem connections?

### Section 4: Milestones
Structure as: **Milestone → Deliverable → Timeline → Amount requested**

Example:
| Milestone | Deliverable | Timeline | Amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| M1: Core SDK | Public GitHub repo with working alpha | Week 6 | $8,000 |
| M2: Docs + testing | Full docs site, 90% test coverage | Week 12 | $8,000 |
| M3: Mainnet launch | Live on mainnet, 10 projects integrated | Week 20 | $9,000 |

### Section 5: Budget breakdown
Be specific. "Development: $20,000" will get pushback. Break it down by milestone and role.

---

## What makes a Foundation application stand out

- **Prior contributions** — answered questions on Discord, filed issues, shipped open-source code
- **Ecosystem alignment** — you understand Solana's architecture (accounts model, rent, compute units)
- **Realistic milestones** — 3-month timelines, not 6-month vague roadmaps
- **Public proof of work** — GitHub, Superteam bounty wins, hackathon placements

---

## Common rejection reasons

- No prior Solana experience visible on GitHub or LinkedIn
- Milestones are vague ("complete development", "launch product")
- Budget doesn't match scope (too high with no justification)
- Problem already solved by a funded project
- Application reads like it was written in 10 minutes

---

## Timeline expectations

- Submit → First response: 2–4 weeks
- First response → Decision: 2–4 more weeks
- Decision → First payment: 1–2 weeks (milestone-gated after that)

Apply to Superteam simultaneously — don't wait on Foundation to hear back.
