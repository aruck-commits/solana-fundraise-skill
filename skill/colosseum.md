# Colosseum — Hackathons & Accelerator

Colosseum runs the official Solana hackathons and operates an accelerator for the best teams. It's the highest-signal credentialing path in the ecosystem — a Colosseum win opens doors that grants alone don't.

---

## Two paths

### 1. Colosseum Hackathons
Online, open-registration hackathons running multiple times a year. Anyone can enter.

- **Prize pool:** $1M+ total across recent hackathons, split across tracks
- **Tracks:** typically DeFi, Infrastructure, AI/DePIN, Consumer, DAOs & Governance
- **Team size:** Solo or team (2-5 recommended)
- **Duration:** 3–4 weeks build time
- **What you need to submit:** Working demo + pitch deck + short video + GitHub repo

**Recent hackathons:** Radar, Breakout, Frontier — check colosseum.org for current cycle.

### 2. Colosseum Accelerator
Selective cohort for the strongest hackathon teams. Not a separate application — you get invited based on hackathon performance.

- **What you get:** $250K–$1M investment (equity or token), mentorship, investor network, Solana Foundation backing
- **Commitment:** Full-time, typically 3–6 month program
- **Location:** Mostly remote, with in-person sprints
- **Acceptance rate:** Very low — think top 1–2% of hackathon entrants

## Hackathon strategy — how to actually place

Most teams that enter Colosseum hackathons lose because they build the wrong thing the right way. Here's what actually works:

### Pick the right track
- Don't pick the most crowded track (usually DeFi)
- AI/DePIN and Consumer have had fewer quality submissions historically
- Pick the track where your idea is genuinely novel, not where you think you can compete

### The demo matters more than the code
Judges see hundreds of submissions. Your demo video is your pitch. Invest 20% of your time in it.

A good demo video:
- Shows the product working (not a slide deck)
- Has a human narrating — no AI voiceover
- Opens with the problem, not the solution
- Is under 3 minutes

### GitHub repo requirements
- Clean README with setup instructions
- Working install path (judges will try to run it)
- MIT licensed
- No copied boilerplate without attribution

### What judges score on
1. **Innovation** — is this genuinely new in Solana?
2. **Technical execution** — does it actually work?
3. **Market potential** — could this be a real company?
4. **Team** — do these people look like they can keep building?
5. **Presentation** — can they communicate clearly?

## If you've never done a Colosseum hackathon

Start with the next online hackathon as a learning round. Your goal for round 1 is:
- Finish and submit something (most teams don't)
- Get feedback from judges
- Build your public GitHub presence

Placing in the top 50% once makes your second hackathon dramatically better.

---

## Combining Colosseum with grants

Best sequence:
1. Win a Superteam Earn bounty or two (builds credibility)
2. Enter a Colosseum hackathon (even if you don't place, you have a project)
3. Use the hackathon project as the basis for a Foundation grant application
4. If you place well → accelerator invitation

This is the fastest path from zero to funded in the Solana ecosystem.
