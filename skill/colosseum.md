# Colosseum — Hackathons & Accelerator

Colosseum runs the official Solana hackathons and operates an accelerator for the best teams. A Colosseum win is the highest-signal credentialing path in the ecosystem — it opens doors that grants alone don't.

---

## Two paths

### 1. Colosseum Hackathons
Online, open-registration hackathons running twice a year. Anyone can enter.

**Most recent: Frontier Hackathon (April 6 – May 11, 2026)**
- Total pool: $2.75M (prizes + pre-seed investment)
- Grand Champion: $30,000
- Top 20 startups: $10,000 each
- Best university team: $10,000
- Best public goods project: $10,000
- Duration: 5 weeks

**Format (as of 2026):** No tracks or bounties — one open competition judged on product impact. The best submission wins, regardless of category.

**Previous hackathons:** Radar, Breakout, Frontier — check colosseum.org for the next cycle. Two per year.

### 2. Colosseum Accelerator
Selective cohort for the strongest hackathon teams. Not a separate application — you get considered based on hackathon performance.

- **Investment:** $250,000 pre-seed funding
- **Up to 10 startups** accepted per cohort
- **Commitment:** Full-time
- **What you get:** Funding, mentorship, investor network, Solana Foundation backing
- **Acceptance rate:** Top ~1% of hackathon entrants

## Hackathon strategy — how to actually place

Most teams lose because they build the wrong thing the right way.

### The demo matters more than the code
Judges see hundreds of submissions. Your demo video is your pitch. Invest 20% of your time in it.

A good demo video:
- Shows the product working — not a slide deck
- Human narrating — no AI voiceover
- Opens with the problem, not the solution
- Under 3 minutes

### GitHub repo requirements
- Clean README with setup instructions
- Working install path (judges will try to run it)
- MIT licensed
- No copied boilerplate without attribution

### What judges score on (Frontier 2026 criteria)
1. **Product impact** — does this solve something real and significant?
2. **Technical execution** — does it actually work?
3. **Business potential** — could this be a real company?
4. **Team** — do these people look like they can keep building?

### The "most impactful" lens
Since Frontier removed tracks, the question is simple: what's the most impactful thing you can build for the Solana ecosystem right now? Cross-chain, AI, DePIN, consumer — anything goes. Pick where your idea is genuinely novel.

## If you've never done a Colosseum hackathon

Start with the next hackathon as a learning round. Your goal for round 1:
- Finish and submit (most teams don't)
- Get feedback
- Build your public GitHub presence

The best Colosseum winner competed in 4 hackathons before taking the grand prize. Repetition compounds.

---

## Combining Colosseum with grants

Best sequence:
1. Win Superteam Earn bounties (builds credibility)
2. Enter a Colosseum hackathon — even without placing, you have a project
3. Use the project as the basis for a Foundation grant application
4. If you place → accelerator consideration → $250K

This is the fastest path from zero to funded in the Solana ecosystem.
