# Finding a Co-Founder or Team

Some grant programs — especially Colosseum — strongly favor teams over solo founders. This file covers how to find collaborators in the Solana ecosystem before applying or entering a hackathon.

---

## When you actually need a co-founder

Not every project needs one. Be honest about what's missing:

**You probably need a co-founder if:**
- You're applying to Colosseum and you're non-technical (no working demo = no entry)
- Your project requires skills you don't have and can't learn in 4 weeks
- You're planning to raise VC after grants — investors prefer teams
- You've tried to build alone twice and keep stalling

**You probably don't need one if:**
- You can ship a working prototype alone
- You're applying for Superteam or Foundation grants (solo is fine)
- You're still at idea stage — find a co-founder after you have something to show

## Colosseum Co-Founder Matching

Colosseum launched a dedicated co-founder matching platform in 2026.

**What it is:** A platform for serious crypto founders to find a technical or business co-founder. Focused on Solana ecosystem builders.

**How to use it:**
1. Go to colosseum.org (look for "Co-Founder Matching" in the nav)
2. Create a profile with your background, what you're building, and what you're looking for
3. Browse other profiles and reach out directly
4. Colosseum also facilitates introductions for high-signal profiles

**What makes a strong co-founder matching profile:**
- Specific about what you're building (not "looking to build in DeFi")
- Clear about what you bring (design, GTM, community, domain expertise)
- Clear about what you need (Rust dev, frontend, mobile)
- Link to your GitHub, Superteam Earn, X — show you're real

## Other places to find collaborators

**Superteam Discord**
The best place to find Solana-native talent. Every regional chapter has builders looking for projects.

- Post in #looking-for-team or #build-with-me channels
- Be specific: "Building [X], need a Rust dev with Anchor experience, 10-15 hrs/week for 3 months"
- Check who's winning bounties in the skills you need — they're proven

**Colosseum Discord**
Active during hackathon cycles. #team-formation channel fills up fast when a hackathon is announced.

**Solana Tech Discord**
More technical, fewer GTM people. Good for finding engineers.

**IRL events**
Web3 Mumbai, Superteam India events, Solana Hacker Houses — the best co-founder relationships start in person. One weekend hackathon with someone tells you more than 10 Zoom calls.

**X (Twitter)**
Search "building on Solana" + filter by people you follow. Reply to builders whose work you respect with a genuine comment. DM if there's a natural fit.

## What to say when reaching out

**In a Discord channel:**
```
Looking for a co-founder / collaborator for [project name].

What I'm building: [one sentence]
What I need: [specific skill — Rust dev, frontend, design]
Time commitment: [hours/week, duration]
What I bring: [your skills + any traction — community, users, prior grants]

Reply here or DM me.
```

**In a direct DM:**
```
Hey [Name], saw your work on [specific project/bounty] — really liked 
how you handled [specific thing].

I'm building [project], currently at [stage]. Looking for someone with 
your background to work on [specific part].

Would you be open to a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit?
```

## How to evaluate a potential co-founder fast

You don't have weeks to evaluate someone before a hackathon deadline. Here's the fast version:

**One small project first**
Before committing, do one small task together — a weekend build, a shared doc, a quick prototype. How they work under pressure tells you everything.

**Three questions that matter:**
1. What have you shipped? (Links or it didn't happen)
2. What's your availability realistically? (People overestimate)
3. What happens if we disagree on a major decision? (How they answer matters more than what they say)

**Green flags:**
- They've shipped and maintained something publicly
- They're specific about what they want to build — not just "open to anything"
- They follow up when they say they will

**Red flags:**
- No public work to show
- Vague about time commitment
- More interested in the grant money than the problem

## Equity and contribution conversations

For hackathons, this is simple — Colosseum prizes go to the registered team. Discuss split upfront, before you submit.

For longer-term projects:
- Have the equity conversation before you apply for grants, not after you get funded
- A simple written agreement (even a DM screenshot) is better than a verbal understanding
- If you're planning to raise VC, talk to a lawyer before finalizing any agreement

## Going solo when you can't find the right person

If you've looked and can't find the right co-founder, don't force it. A solo founder who ships beats a team that fights.

For Colosseum specifically — solo founders can and do win. The Frontier 2026 hackathon had solo winners. What matters is the product, not the team size.

If you're solo:
- Be explicit about it in your application — don't pretend to have a team
- Show you can wear multiple hats: link to design work, code, and written communication
- Plan milestones that are achievable alone in the timeframe
